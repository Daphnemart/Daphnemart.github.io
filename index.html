Recipe

Ingredients for the Sauce

    1. 8 guajillo or new mexico peppers
    2. 1 ancho chile
    3. ¼ of a large onion
    4. 1 large garlic clove
    5. 1 teaspoon kosher salt + ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    6. 2 cups stock (either vegetable, beef, chicken stock will do). Alternatively, you can use water
    7. plus 1 bullion cube (vegetable, beef or chicken).
    8. 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    9. 1 – 8 oz can tomato sauce
    10. Ingredients for the Enchiladas

Enchilada Sauce

    1. Corn tortillas
    2. Oil for frying the tortillas
    3. The filling -For enchiladas rojas de queso, use crumbled queso fresco or cotija.
    4. For beef enchiladas, use shredded beef (follow directions from my Carne Deshebrada
      5. post).
    5. For chicken enchiladas, use homemade shredded chicken or shredded rotisserie chicken.
    6. For a vegan version, fill with Frijoles Refritos or Calabacitas.
    7. Toppings: Shredded lettuce like romaine or iceberg, queso fresco, and radishes

Making the Enchilada Sauce

    1. Remove all seeds and stem from the peppers then rinse under cold water to remove any dust.
    2. In a medium pot, add the chiles, onions and garlic clove.
    3. Add enough water to cover the chiles.
    4. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 20 minutes or until very soft and pliable.
    5. Rest chiles in the water for another 10 to 15 minutes to cool off and finish hydrating the chiles .
    6. Transfer chiles to a blender or food processor and add 2 cups broth.
    7. Puree for about 1 minute.
    8. Note: you can do this in two different batches if the blender isn’t big enough for all the ingredients at once.
    9. Strain the pureed chiles to remove the remaining seeds and skins (tip: push the chile mix with a spoon to get the most out of them).
    10. In a large saucepan, heat the 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the pureed chiles and tomato sauce.
    11. Cook, stirring often for about 10 minutes.
    12. Season with salt and pepper according to taste.

Making the Enchiladas

  • Add about ¼ cup of oil to a small or medium skillet.
  • Heat on medium-high heat. When the oil is very hot (but not smoky), add one tortilla at the time and cook each side for about 4 to 5 seconds or until tortilla is lightly fried.
    • Note: The tortillas should still be pliable for folding so make sure they’re not fried too long.
  • Check out this video on how I fry the tortilla lightly.
  • As you fry each tortilla, drain any excess oil by tapping it into the pan then place on a plate with a paper towel.
  • Repeat with remaining tortillas and add more oil if necessary.
